Kaddy Benyon

Invited Poet, The Polar Museum, Scott Polar Research Institute

Kaddy Benyon was born in Cambridge in 1973. She worked as a television scriptwriter for a number of years, penning over 70 episodes of Hollyoaks and Grange Hill, as well as three young adult novels. After completing an MA in Creative Writing, Kaddy won the Crashaw Prize with the manuscript for her first collection of poetry, Milk Fever, (Salt Publishing, 2012).

Kaddy is a Granta New Poet and was highly commended in the Forward Prizes in 2013. She is currently Invited Poet at The Polar Museum and was funded by Arts Council England to research and write her second collection, Call Her Alaska, a contemporary re-imagining of The Snow Queen.

‘White on White,’ specially commissioned for The Thing Is…, explores the obssession with flying shared by Andrée and Brancusi. Kaddy had made a further selection of work of hers that has a polar connection.
