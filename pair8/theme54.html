Nils Strindberg (1872–1897)

Nils Strindberg was a Swedish physicist and photographer. He was invited by S. A. Andrée to accompany him, along with Knut Fraenkel, on an ill-fated attempt to reach the North Pole by hot air balloon in 1897. Nils had recently become engaged to a Swedish governess, Anna Charlier. He gave her a stuffed carrier pigeon as a parting gift before he set sail for the Arctic. Anna waited for Nils to return from the expedition for thirteen years, fastidiously recording every piece of news coverage in four scrapbooks. She eventually married American, Gilbert Hawtrey and moved to England, but was never able to forget Nils; her home was filled with portraits of him. The remains of Salomon August Andrée, Nils Strindberg and Knut Fraenkel were eventually recovered by the crew of a whaling vessel on White Island in 1930. Anna died in 1949, leaving specific instructions for her heart to be removed and buried beside the ashes of her first love, Nils Strindberg, in Stockholm.
