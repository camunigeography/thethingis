Sedgwick Museum of Earth Sciences

The Sedgwick Museum of Earth Sciences is the oldest of the University of Cambridge museums, its origin lies in a collection of 9,400 rocks, minerals, fossils and archaeological artefacts bequeathed to the University by Dr. John Woodward in 1728. Since then the collection has grown to approximately 2 million items that help us to discover and understand the 4.5 billion year geological history of the Earth.
