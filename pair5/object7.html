Fitzwilliam Museum

The largest of the University of Cambridge's museums, the Fitzwilliam was founded in 1816, from the bequest of Richard, 7th Viscount Fitzwilliam of Merrion. Lord Fitzwilliam left the University his extensive collections including manuscripts and musical scores, rare books, Old Master paintings, prints and other works of art.
