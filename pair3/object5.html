Whipple Museum of the History of Science

The Whipple Museum was founded in 1944 when Robert Stewart Whipple presented his scientific instruments to the University of Cambridge. The museum's collection, which is particularly strong in material dating from the 17th to the 19th centuries, includes scientific instruments, apparatus, models, pictures, prints, photographs, books and other material related to the history of science.
