Andrew Atkin

Dr Andrew Atkin is an Australian historian who specialises in analysing and evaluating Antarctic scientific practice during the Heroic Age, when expeditions led by Scott, Shackleton, Filchner and others began coordinated scientific research in Antarctica. His PhD thesis was on, ‘Drivers of scientific success; an analysis of terrestrial magnetism on the Discovery Antarctic expedition, 1901–04’. Dr Atkin is currently involved in the retrieval and reformatting of historical scientific data, making it available for current research projects.
